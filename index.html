Healthcare · Product x AI

Automating document workflows for doctors

At Doctolib, I worked on AI-powered flows to reduce the time doctors spend on repetitive administrative tasks, using OCR and ML to structure medical documents at scale.

  • Problem discovery with doctors & assistants
  • MVP definition with data scientists & engineers

Sports betting · Product x AI

Clarifying betting rules & automating decisions

At Betclic, I worked on structuring betting rules to serve both the front-end UI and an AI companion, with a strong focus on support automation and market standardisation.

  • Rule system to power tooltips & chatbots
  • Use cases around web scraping & auto-result
  • Target: reduce support costs & confusion

About

A bit more about who I am and what shapes the way I work.

Short bio

I’m a French engineer with a background in data science and product management. I’ve worked across healthcare, sports betting and consulting, and I love turning complex, ambiguous problems into clear product decisions that teams can rally around.

Outside of work, three passions shape how I think and build: chess, kitesurfing, and climate & the environment.

Chess

Chess taught me to think in plans rather than single moves, evaluate trade-offs and stay calm with incomplete information. In product work, that means strategy over feature lists and clear long-term thinking.

Kitesurfing

Kitesurfing is a masterclass in iteration and risk management: you fall a lot, adjust quickly, and learn to respect constraints. That’s how I like to run AI experiments and product discovery.

Environment

As a “Fresque du Climat” facilitator, I care deeply about climate and orders of magnitude. I try to focus on 10x levers rather than cosmetic optimisations, both in climate and in product work.
